Recognize Critical Emergency Symptoms

These symptoms require immediate veterinary attention. Do not wait - contact emergency services immediately.

Breathing Difficulties

  • Gasping or open-mouth breathing
  • Blue or pale gums
  • Excessive panting when not hot
  • Choking or gagging sounds

IMMEDIATE ACTION: Keep airways clear, get to vet immediately

Neurological Signs

  • Seizures or convulsions
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Inability to stand or walk
  • Severe disorientation

IMMEDIATE ACTION: Keep pet safe, time seizures, seek emergency care

Trauma & Bleeding

  • Severe bleeding that won't stop
  • Hit by car or major trauma
  • Visible broken bones
  • Deep wounds or lacerations

IMMEDIATE ACTION: Apply pressure to bleeding, transport carefully

Poisoning Signs

  • Vomiting or diarrhea
  • Excessive drooling
  • Lethargy or weakness
  • Loss of coordination

IMMEDIATE ACTION: Call poison control, bring substance packaging

Bloat/GDV

  • Distended, hard abdomen
  • Unproductive retching
  • Restlessness and pacing
  • Rapid breathing

IMMEDIATE ACTION: Emergency surgery needed - get to vet NOW

Heat Stroke

  • Excessive panting
  • Bright red gums
  • Vomiting or diarrhea
  • Collapse or weakness

IMMEDIATE ACTION: Cool gradually with water, get to vet

Emergency First Aid Protocols

Step-by-step emergency procedures to stabilize your pet before veterinary care. These are temporary measures - always seek professional help.

Pet CPR Protocol

1

Check Responsiveness

Tap pet gently and call their name. Check for breathing and pulse.

2

Position Pet

Place on firm surface, right side down. Extend head and neck.

3

Chest Compressions

Small pets: Use one hand. Large pets: Use both hands. Compress 1/3 to 1/2 chest width.

4

Rescue Breathing

Close mouth, breathe into nose. 30 compressions : 2 breaths ratio.

Continue until pet responds or you reach veterinary care

Choking Emergency

1

Open Mouth

Carefully open pet's mouth and look for visible objects. Do NOT blindly reach in.

2

Remove Object

If visible, use tweezers or pliers to carefully remove. Avoid pushing deeper.

3

Back Blows

Small pets: Hold upside down, strike between shoulder blades. Large pets: Lift hind legs, strike back.

4

Check & Repeat

Check mouth again. Repeat back blows if needed. Get to vet immediately.

If unsuccessful after 3 attempts, get to emergency vet immediately

Prevention & Preparedness

The best emergency is the one that never happens. Take these proactive steps to keep your pet safe.

Regular Health Monitoring

  • Schedule annual wellness exams
  • Keep vaccinations current
  • Monitor weight and appetite changes
  • Learn your pet's normal behavior
